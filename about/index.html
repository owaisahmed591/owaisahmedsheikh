My Story

How I Got Into SEO

I started learning SEO in 2019 out of curiosity. I had a small blog at the time, and I wanted to know why some articles ranked on page one while others were buried on page five. That question led me down a deep rabbit hole.

Within a few months, I was helping friends and family with their websites. A cousin had a small clothing store in Karachi and could not figure out why her competitors were showing up on Google but she was not. I fixed her technical issues, optimized her pages, and built some backlinks. Within four months, she was on page one for her main search terms and the store started getting calls from Google.

That was the moment I realized SEO was what I wanted to do full time.

Since then, I have worked with restaurants, law firms, e-commerce stores, medical clinics, and service businesses across Karachi and the rest of Pakistan. I have also built the SEO Readiness Chrome extension, a tool used by link builders worldwide to check site quality before placing backlinks.

My Approach

What I Believe About SEO

SEO should make business sense Rankings only matter if they bring you customers. I focus on keywords that have real commercial value for your business, not just high search volume with no buying intent.
Transparency over everything I will never send you a report full of graphs you do not understand. Every month, I explain in plain language what changed, why it changed, and what the next steps are.
Quality over shortcuts Black hat tricks may work for a few months. Then Google updates its algorithm and your site disappears. I only use methods that are safe long-term and follow Google's guidelines.
Local knowledge matters Ranking in Karachi is different from ranking in London or New York. I know the local search patterns, the local competition, and what makes Karachi customers click.
Core Belief
The best SEO is helpful content on a fast, well-structured site, backed by links from real websites. Everything else is secondary.
